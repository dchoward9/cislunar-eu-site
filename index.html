Melagen Labs → Multi-State Site Selection

Melagen Labs — $4.5M Public-Private Partnership for Radiation Testing Infrastructure

The Cislunar Group led a multi-state site selection process for Melagen Labs across four states and multiple cities before identifying Taylor, Texas as the optimal location. The engagement culminated in a $4.5 million Economic Development Performance Agreement — Taylor's first-ever public-private partnership of this kind — for a nationally significant radiation testing facility. 120 days from concept to announcement.

ProcessMulti-state site selection across four states and multiple cities before final selection in Taylor, Texas
Deal$4.5M Economic Development Performance Agreement — Taylor's first-ever public-private partnership of this kind
Founder roleStephen McCall led the full engagement — economic development strategy, public-private partnership architecture, and government relations — from inception through board approval
FacilityCo-60 Radiation Effects Testing & Qualification Center addressing a national radiation testing bottleneck across space, defense, semiconductor, and advanced electronics

Firefly Aerospace — Virginia Launch Capability After Failed $380K Site Selection Engagement

During his tenure as Head of Government Affairs at Firefly Aerospace, Stephen led the state engagement and site strategy that supported Firefly's Virginia expansion after a traditional site selection engagement failed. The result was a faster path to launch capability at Wallops Island — one of the founder experiences that shaped why The Cislunar Group was built.
